Handmade Moroccan rugs are typically crafted from natural materials such as wool, cotton, and sometimes jute, ensuring durability and a unique texture.

How can I tell if a Moroccan rug is genuine?

Genuine Moroccan rugs often feature unique, handwoven patterns and irregularities, as well as natural dyes. Checking for the artisan's signature or label from a reputable seller like Kechart can also help verify authenticity.

There are several styles of Moroccan rugs, including Berber rugs, vintage rugs, and boho rugs, each showcasing distinct designs, patterns, and cultural influences.
