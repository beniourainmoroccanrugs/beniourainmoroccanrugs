What is natural wool, and why is it important in Moroccan craftsmanship?

Natural wool is derived from sheep and is valued in Moroccan craftsmanship for its durability, warmth, and versatility. It plays a crucial role in traditional crafts, reflecting the cultural heritage and artisanal skills of Morocco.

What types of natural wool are commonly used in Moroccan crafts?

Common types of natural wool used in Moroccan crafts include sheep wool, camel wool, and goat wool (cashmere and mohair). Each type has unique qualities that contribute to the final product's texture and insulation properties.

What is the process of creating Moroccan wool felt crafts?

The process involves washing and carding the wool to separate the fibers, followed by matting the wool together using water, soap, and agitation to create felt. This material is then shaped, dyed, and finished into various handcrafted items.

How do Moroccan artisans source and prepare natural wool for crafting?

Moroccan artisans typically source natural wool from local shepherds and farms. The preparation process includes washing, carding, and dyeing the wool to enhance its colors and textures before it is transformed into crafts.
